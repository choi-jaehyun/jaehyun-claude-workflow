Referee Report

Addressed to the author of the study (your identity is hidden unless you give it away in the report). Typically does not include your recommendation, just a summary of your assessment. Covers topics like strengths/weakness that make it appropriate for the journal.
Structure
Opening: Paragraph or so explaining in your own words what the paper does. Big picture takeaway about what was good/bad.
Major Comments
Enumerated list of comments that you think are substantive.
Longer points on major aspects of the paper that affect its quality.
Minor Comments
Enumerated list of spelling/Grammar/corrections and things the author maybe wants to change but is not substantive.
Cover Letter to the Editor

Includes and explains a recommendation (Accept, Revise and Resubmit, Reject) and explains your reasoning. The editor may or may not follow your recommendation.
Can include things that are not raised in the review itself. You might think the paper is fine, but "not good enough" for the journal's usual standards.
